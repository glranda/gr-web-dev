Structures Unlimited

The Project

Structures Unlimited is a structural engineering firm based in Northern Illinois. In 2017, they contacted me about updating their web presence. They were looking for something sleek and modern, but also clear and easy to comprehend. I was already aware of one challenge with sites like these, which is keeping the user engaged with the content. What about the site makes the user care enough to read about the past projects? Or read the mission statement? How can I build a brand that is both modern and interesting enough to keep users engaged but doesn't potentially alienate an older client base? I started by conducting a short interview with the CEO.

The Layout

After learning about the typical customer for Structures Unlimited, I decided that it would be best to design a site that features bold and easy to read text, large spacing and proportions, and a subdued and professional color scheme. This way, a user of any age or technical know-how will have no problem reading or navigating through the site. We also decided that it shouldn't be too wordy. It should be just enough to explain itself without being overwhelming.

See the InVision Prototype

The Original Site

The new website for Structures Unlimited was rolled out in early 2018 to very favorable reactions from the clients and from their customers. It features new brand colors, a more clear heirarchy, and updated content. The old website for Structures Unlimited, while simple and straight-forward, was lacking a clear path through the various pages in the site. I feel that the increased vertical negative space encourages users to explore and learn more about the company, and the updated typography helped to improve readability.
