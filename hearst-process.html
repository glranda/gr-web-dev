Hearst Newspapers

In 2019 I was brought on to the engineering team at Hearst in New York. It was my job to get integrated as quickly as possible to help deal with a rapidly growing backlog and a long list of clients. Working on a large team made up of Front-End, Back-End, Designers, and Product Owners, I worked to quickly learn a proprietary PHP based content management system...

What I did:

  • Redesigned several client websites used SCSS mixins and includes, LESS nesting, and CSS3
  • Utilized JavaScript and JQuery to improve page load performance with ‘lazy loading’, and used HTML5 elements such as ‘srcset’ to lower image sizes
  • Maintained, improved, and added new features to a custom-made PHP based Content Management System
  • Lead a project that improved the ADA compliance of all 40 websites on the Hearst platform by utilizing semantic HTML5, aria roles and labels, and JavaScript to improve tab-ability
  • Worked with 3rd party vendors to integrate new features into the existing codebase and to handle any issues or maintenance that followed

Improving Page Load at Hearst

Using a mix of JavaScript, HTML5, and responsive breakpoints I was able to help the page performance across the Hearst Platform. I utilized a technique called 'Lazy Loading' where JavaScript only loads images that are visible to the viewer, everything else below will not load until it is needed.
